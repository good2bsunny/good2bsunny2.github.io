CIS 1300 Networking Concepts

The course goal is to explore the components of networks and network designs; communications hardware and the interconnection of servers and clients within LANs and WANs; network architectures, standards, protocols and access methods used within intranets and the Internet; functions of network operating systems; and centralized computing, client/server and peer-to-peer environments, their services and their program-to-program communication protocols.

CIS 1200 Intro to Database Systems

Upon successful completion of this course, students are expected to learn concepts involved with multi-user database systems, data models, relational database models, entity relationship modeling, normalization of database tables, transaction management and concurrency, and business intelligence and data warehouses. Students are also expected to learn how to code and develop database language involving basic SQL and some advanced SQL.

This course prepares students to use the concepts and skills in this course in many areas of business that use and leverage information technology and management. It also prepares students to take more advanced database courses in preparation for a career in information technology.
